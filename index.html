Our Mission

The USBlogger

Real News. Every Angle. No Agenda.

Why We Exist

The American media landscape is fractured. Pick a channel, and you already know what opinion you're going to get. Pick a website, and the headlines are written to tell you what to feel before you've even read the first sentence. That's not journalism — that's noise.

The USBlogger was built on a simple belief: the American people are smart enough to make their own decisions. They don't need to be told what to think. They need to be given the real picture — all of it, from every angle — and trusted to figure it out.

That's what we do. Every single day.

"The USBlogger is an independent American news analysis publication. We cover politics, economy, military, technology, markets, and the issues shaping everyday life — synthesized from across the political spectrum, delivered with balance and no agenda."
— Michael Monreal, Founder & Independent Media Analyst

How We Work

Before a single word gets written, our system analyzes coverage from 20 major American news and financial sources — across the political spectrum, across formats, across regions. CNN and Fox News. NPR and MSNBC. The Wall Street Journal and Reuters. AP and Bloomberg. All of it goes in.

Then we write one article. Not their version. Not a partisan take. A centralized version — the one that reflects what's actually being reported, stripped of the spin, and written in plain English that any American can read and understand.

We cover six categories every day: Government & Politics, Economy & Jobs, Military & Veterans, Rights & Freedoms, Technology & AI, and Markets & Finance. Each one gets the same standard: both sides, full context, and an honest look at where things actually stand.

⚖️
No Bias. Ever.

We don't have a party. We don't have a political donor. We don't have a corporate owner telling us what to cover. The only standard is the truth, and the only loyalty is to the American people.

🔍
All Sides, One Story

Every article we publish goes through a full cross-reference of sources from across the spectrum. The result isn't a compromise — it's the clearest picture of what's actually happening.

🤝
We Trust You

We don't tell you what to think, what to believe, or how to vote. We lay out the facts, present every perspective fairly, and trust you — because you've earned it — to make your own informed decisions.

On Military & Veterans

The men and women who serve this country deserve more than a soundbite. Our Military & Veterans section is built differently — it leads with active operations, current deployments, and what's actually happening on the ground. Not headlines designed to score political points. Real reporting on real missions, sourced from official statements, Pentagon briefings, and the voices of veterans themselves.

No glorifying. No politicizing. Just the facts, with the respect that section has always deserved.

Editorial Standards

Every article published on The USBlogger is original analysis. We do not reproduce content from other outlets. We do not quote without full attribution. We do not make claims we cannot verify from multiple sources. If something is uncertain, we say so. If something is opinion, it is labeled as such.

We take copyright seriously. We take defamation law seriously. We take our responsibility to readers seriously. Every piece goes through a standard that would hold up to editorial and legal review — because our readers deserve nothing less.

★ Our Commitment to You ★

Editorial Standards

The principles that govern every word published on The USBlogger

Every publication makes choices — what to cover, how to frame it, whose voice to amplify. At The USBlogger, we believe you deserve to know exactly how those choices get made. These aren't aspirational guidelines collecting dust on a shelf. They are the operating rules of this publication, applied to every article, every day.

⚖️

Balance & Fairness

Every story with two legitimate sides gets both sides — with equal weight, equal care, and equal space. We do not editorialize about which side is right. We do not bury the perspective we disagree with in the final paragraph. We present, you decide.

If a political figure on the left does something worth reporting, we cover it. If a political figure on the right does the same, we cover it the same way. The standard doesn't change based on who's involved.

🔍

Source Standards

Every article on The USBlogger is synthesized from at least 8 to 20 major American and international news sources, including AP, Reuters, NPR, PBS, CNN, Fox News, NBC, ABC, CBS, Wall Street Journal, Bloomberg, CNBC, and others.

We never rely on a single source. We never publish a claim that cannot be cross-referenced. When sources conflict, we say so. When facts are disputed, we label them as disputed.

✍️

Original Reporting

Every article published here is original analysis — not a reprint, not a paraphrase of another outlet's work, not a press release dressed up as journalism. We read the sources, then we write our own assessment of what they mean for Americans.

We never reproduce copyrighted material. We never copy sentences or structures from other publications. If we quote a public figure, we include their name, title, and the exact context in which they said it.

🛡️

Legal & Ethical Responsibility

We do not defame. We do not publish unverified allegations as fact. We do not target private individuals. Every claim we make is grounded in documented actions, official statements, or verified public record.

Opinion is clearly labeled as opinion. Analysis is clearly labeled as analysis. We do not disguise one as the other. When we get something wrong, we correct it — prominently, immediately, and without hedging.

🇺🇸

Independence

The USBlogger has no corporate owners, no political donors, no advertising partners, and no editorial board beholden to outside interests. Michael Monreal founded this publication to fill a specific gap: honest, multi-source American news that trusts readers to think for themselves.

No party, no ideology, no interest group influences what we cover or how. Our only obligation is to the reader.

🔄

Corrections Policy

We make mistakes. Every publication does. The difference is what you do next. When an error is discovered — whether by our team or by a reader — we correct it in the article immediately, note the correction clearly at the top of the piece, and never quietly edit history.

If you find an error in anything we've published, contact us. We take every correction seriously and respond to every credible report.

💡

AI & Technology

The USBlogger uses AI as a reporting and writing tool — not as a replacement for editorial judgment. Every article is generated from real-time source synthesis and reviewed against editorial guidelines before publication. The voice, the standards, and the accountability belong to Michael Monreal.

We are transparent about this. We believe AI-assisted journalism, held to rigorous editorial standards, can produce reporting that is faster, broader, and more balanced than any single human journalist working alone.

👥

Reader Relationship

We work for you. Not for clicks, not for engagement metrics, not for shares. We write the article a thoughtful, busy American actually needs — complete enough to inform, honest enough to trust, and clear enough to actually read.

We never sensationalize. We never use fear to drive traffic. We never tell you what to think. We give you what happened, what it means, and what both sides are actually saying.

★

The USBlogger Pledge

We will never publish something we know to be false. We will never hide a perspective because it's inconvenient. We will never put our thumb on the scale. And when we fall short of these standards — as every publication eventually does — we will say so plainly and make it right.

— Michael Monreal, Founder & Independent Media Analyst

★ Legal ★

Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2025

What We Collect

The USBlogger does not collect personal information from readers. We do not require registration, login, or any account creation to read our content. We do not store your name, email address, IP address, or any identifying information on our servers.

If you choose to contact us via email, we retain that correspondence solely to respond to your inquiry. We do not add you to any mailing list without your explicit consent.

Cookies & Local Storage

The USBlogger uses your browser's local storage to cache today's articles for faster loading. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to any server. No tracking cookies are set. No third-party advertising cookies are used.

Third-Party Services

We use the Anthropic API to generate article content. This involves sending editorial prompts to Anthropic's servers. No reader data is included in these requests. We use Supabase for article storage. Supabase's privacy practices are governed by their own policy.

We use Google Fonts to load typefaces. Google may log font requests. We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any behavioral tracking tools.

Your Rights

Because we do not collect personal data, there is nothing to delete, correct, or export. If you have any privacy-related questions, contact us at the address below.
